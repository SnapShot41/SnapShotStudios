Out of the Lab

This is the work that doesn’t behave. The ideas that showed up uninvited. The assignments that turned into experiments. Out of the Lab is where I push, break, rebuild, and chase whatever sparks. Nothing here fits a category — and that’s exactly the point.

Reflective Light

A single low light cuts across the table, filtered through six mason jars acting as makeshift prisms. Dried leaves sit in intentional pockets, breaking the path of the eye and interrupting the brightness. The result is part experiment, part accident, part quiet little universe of light and texture.

Project Title Two

This one started with me tilting a deep red stone and blasting light through the blue candle holder just to see if I could coax those star shapes onto the surface. When the stars finally showed up, I wasn’t done — I wanted them in the bottom holder too. So I grabbed a mirror, bounced the light back into the setup, and forced the stars to land exactly where I wanted them. The blue confetti? That was just me adding a little chaos to the scene because it felt too behaved without it.

Still Life

What began as a class still‑life quickly turned into a playful product concept. I placed the cologne bottle inside the lemonade pitcher and added fresh lemon leaves I picked myself to keep the scene feeling authentic and vibrant. The result blends commercial polish with a touch of creative experimentation.

Shiny Metal

Instead of photographing a predictable metal object, I reached for the Japanese sword I brought back from my travels. I paired it with blossoms from a nearby tree — a nod to cherry blossoms — and layered in a piece of burlap to warm the scene. The contrast between steel and petals, sharpness and softness, felt like the perfect way to elevate a simple shiny‑metal project into something with soul.
